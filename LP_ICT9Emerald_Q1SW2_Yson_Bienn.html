Crisostomo Ibarra

 he protagonist of Jose Rizal's book Noli me Tangere is Juan Crisostomo Ibarra. Son of Don Rafael, his full name is Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin. He is commonly referred to as Ibarra in the book.

Maria Clara

 The mestiza protagonist of José Rizal's work Noli Me Tángere, is named Maria Clara. Her name and persona have now come to symbolize the conventional, feminine ideal in Filipino culture.

Padre Damaso

 The real father of Maria Clara was Padre Damaso, a former town curate in San Diego. The man attempted to keep his daughter and Crisostomo Ibarra apart for Maria Clara's benefit by opposing their marriage.

Teniete Guevarra

 Miguel Guevarra, also known as Teniente Guevarra, was Don Rafael Ibarra's close friend and a seasoned officer in the Guardia Civil. He also attempted to keep Don Rafael's son Crisostomo safe once he returned from Europe because of his admiration for the man.

Capitan Tiago

 Maria Clara's father, Capitan Tiago, was a prominent businessman in San Diego. Capitan Tiago failed to follow the friars' instructions and betrothed his daughter to Crisostomo Ibarra.
